D2MS Retention Project Plan

A simple, staged plan to improve D2MS customer retention.

Simple Aim

Measure Find who stays, pauses, or cancels.
Coach Improve calls and agent habits.
Support Help customers after sign-up.
Align Reward D2MS for retained sales.

Main Project Stages

  • 1. Data Analytics
  • 2. Retention Scorecard
  • 3. First Box Calls
  • 4. Early Life Rescue
  • 5. Commission Model
  • 6. Dashboard
  • 7. Training

Project Roadmap

Stage 1
Data
Measure 28-day retention, 84-day retention, and pauses before box 1.
Stage 2
Scorecard
Listen to calls and score behaviours that drive retained customers.
Stage 3
First Box Calls
Call customers after box 1 to check confidence, clarity, and risk.
Stage 4
Rescue
Flag risky customers early and send help before they cancel.
Stage 5
Commission
Move D2MS rewards toward retained sales, not just sign-ups.
Stage 6
Dashboard
Show simple performance, risks, and next actions.
Stage 7
Training
Turn F2F skills into telephone-based retention skills.

Stage 1: Steps Inside the Steps

1. Set Metrics

  • 28-day retention
  • 84-day retention
  • Paused before first box

2. Pull Data

  • Customer ID
  • Agent

3. Clean Data

  • Check missing dates

4. Analyse

  • By agent
  • By product

  • By payment type

5. Output

  • Risk list
  • Dashboard

Stage 2: Retention Scorecard

Build

  • Clear price
  • Clear product
  • No overpromising
  • Strong next steps

Test

  • Listen to retained calls
  • Listen to churned calls
  • Compare patterns

Use

  • Coach agents
  • Share good call clips
  • Track score vs retention

Stage 3: First Box Follow-Up Calls

Call Timing

  • Call after box 1 arrives
  • Best window: 2-5 days after delivery
  • Keep it helpful, not salesy

Call Checks

  • Did it arrive?
  • Is the customer happy?
  • Do they understand next steps?
  • Any reason they may cancel?

Measure

  • Called vs not called
  • 28-day retention
  • 84-day retention
  • Pause and cancel rate

Stage 4: Early Life Rescue

Risk Flags

  • Paused before box 1
  • Failed payment
  • Short sales call
  • No app launch
  • No dog photo

Rescue Actions

  • Welcome SMS
  • Helpful email
  • Payment reminder
  • Support call

Success Measures

  • Lower early pause rate
  • Lower cancellation rate
  • Higher 28-day retention

Stage 5: D2MS Commission Model

Reward

  • Valid sale
  • Second payment
  • 28-day retention
  • 84-day retention

Protect

  • Reduce reward for early churn
  • Track complaints
  • Track refunds
  • Track payment failures

Goal

  • Fewer weak sales
  • More retained customers
  • Better agency alignment

Stage 6: Dashboard

Show

  • Sales
  • 28-day retention
  • 84-day retention
  • Paused before box 1

Break Down

  • By agent
  • By offer
  • By product
  • By payment type

Avoid

  • Public shame lists
  • Raw rankings only
  • Ignoring lead quality

Stage 7: Training

Take from F2F

  • Trust building
  • Objection handling
  • Confidence
  • Clear close

Turn into Phone Skills

  • Tone
  • Pace
  • Listening
  • Spotting doubt

Train

  • Short sessions
  • Real call clips
  • Role play
  • Weekly refresh

Paused Before First Box Formula

Use this if the sheet has Order 1 Delivery Date and Pause Date.

=IF(AND(PauseDate<>"",Order1DeliveryDate<>"",PauseDate<Order1DeliveryDate),"Yes","No")
